What merchants get

  • USDC + USDT stablecoin checkout
  • Multi-network support (customers choose the supported network)
  • QR code POS for in-store payments
  • Payment links for invoices & remote sales
  • 2% fee added on top (customer pays merchant amount + fee)
  • Receipts via transaction hashes (on-chain proof)

How it works

  1. Merchant enters amount (or creates a payment link).
  2. Olynt generates a QR code and a shareable link.
  3. Customer scans, connects a wallet, and pays.
  4. Customer confirms two transfers: merchant amount + 2% fee to treasury.
  5. Merchant verifies via tx hash on the explorer.
Tip: For best UX, customers should open the pay page inside a wallet browser (MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet) or use WalletConnect when enabled.

Best for

Service businesses — barbers, freelancers, repairs, deliveries
Online sellers — invoices via payment links

Fees

Olynt Pay charges a 2% fee added on top of the merchant amount. Example:

If a merchant charges $100, the customer pays $102 total:
• $100 → merchant wallet
• $2 → Olynt treasury (fee)

Network gas fees are paid by the customer’s wallet (depends on the network).
